Step 1

Getting Started

Open TapShift, click Register, and enter your name, email, and a password. You'll be logged in immediately with a 2-month free trial — no credit card required.

Register an account

Go to the app, click Register, and fill in your name, email address, and a password. hCaptcha is used to prevent bots — just complete the quick challenge.

Explore the three tabs

The app has three main tabs at the bottom: Timer (your tracking buttons), History (calendar and statistics), and Settings (account and preferences).

Set your country in Settings

Head to Settings → Country and choose your federal state (Bundesland). This activates the correct public holiday calendar for your balance calculations.

Step 2

Setting Your Work Target

A target defines how many hours you are contracted to work per day and which days are working days. TapShift uses this to compute your running balance.

Create a target

In Settings → Targets, tap Add Target and give it a name (e.g. "Full-time contract"). Enter the contracted minutes per day for each weekday. For a standard 8 h/day Mon–Fri contract, enter 480 for Monday–Friday and 0 for Saturday and Sunday.

Set a start date

Enter the date your contract started. TapShift will calculate your cumulative balance from that date forward, including any public holidays that reduce your due hours.

Toggle "Exclude public holidays"

If your contract counts public holidays as paid days off (the standard for German employees), enable this option. Due hours for public holidays will be set to zero automatically.

Step 3

Creating Tracking Buttons

Each activity you want to track needs a button. Buttons live on the Timer tab and are the primary way you interact with the app.

Tap "+" to add a button

On the Timer tab, tap the + button. A configuration screen will open.

Choose a name and emoji

Give your button a short label (e.g. "Work") and pick an emoji to make it recognisable at a glance. Good examples: 💼 Work, 🏠 Home Office, ✈️ Business Trip, 🧑‍⚕️ Doctor.

Pick a colour

Assign a colour to each button. Colours appear in the calendar history view, making it easy to see which activity you did on which day.

Enable "Apply break calculation" (recommended)

For any button that represents actual work time, enable this toggle. German law requires a 30-minute break for shifts of 6 h or more and 45 minutes for 9 h or more. TapShift will subtract the correct amount automatically when you stop the timer.

Step 4

Recording Time

Tap a button to start

Tapping a tracking button starts the timer. The button grows slightly and pulses to indicate it's active. The elapsed time is displayed and updates every minute.

Tap again to stop

Tap the active button a second time to stop the timer. The time log is saved locally and synced to the server. If break calculation is enabled, the automatic deduction is applied at this point.

Add entries manually

Forgot to start the timer? Go to History → Add Manual Entry, pick the date and time range, select the button type, and save. Manual entries work exactly the same as timer-based ones.

Edit or delete entries

Tap any day in the calendar view to see its entries. Long-press (or swipe) any entry to edit or delete it. The balance recalculates immediately.

Important

How Break Deduction Works

German law (§ 4 ArbZG) mandates rest breaks. TapShift automates this entirely when Apply break calculation is enabled on a button:

⏰

Shifts of 6 h or more

30 minutes are subtracted from the recorded duration.

⏰⏰

Shifts of 9 h or more

45 minutes are subtracted from the recorded duration.

Example: You start at 08:00 and stop at 17:30 — a raw duration of 9.5 hours. Because it exceeds 9 hours, TapShift records 8 hours 45 minutes (9.5 h − 45 min) as your worked time. No manual entry needed.

Break deduction is applied once per time log based on the total duration, and only on the last day if a shift spans midnight.

Special Days

Logging Special Day Types

Not every day is a normal working day. TapShift tracks the following types, all of which contribute to your balance correctly:

🤒

Sick Day

Logged as a whole-day entry. Due minutes for that day are added to worked minutes — your balance is unaffected.

👶

Child-Sick Day

Same treatment as sick days. Tracked separately so you can see the count in your yearly statistics.

🏖️

Holiday (Urlaub)

Whole-day entry. Counts as worked for balance purposes. Tracked separately from public holidays.

✈️

Business Trip

Logged as a timed entry. Break deduction applies as normal. Counted as a worked day in statistics.

To log a special day, go to the Timer tab, tap Add Entry (or use the manual entry form in History), and select the appropriate type from the dropdown.

Public Holidays

Public Holidays

TapShift fetches the official public holiday calendar for your federal state automatically. Holidays are stored locally and refreshed when they're more than 3 months old.

When a target is configured with Exclude public holidays enabled (recommended for standard German employment contracts), the due hours for those days are automatically set to zero. You'll see the day marked in your calendar but it won't count against you in the balance.

Tip: Different federal states have different public holiday counts. Bavaria (Bayern) has the most; Berlin has fewer. Make sure your state is set correctly in Settings → Country to get an accurate balance.
History

History and Statistics

The History tab gives you a full picture of your working time across different time scales.

Calendar view

A colour-coded calendar shows which days had activity. Tap any day to see a detailed breakdown of all time logs for that day.

Monthly summary

See your total worked hours, due hours, overtime or deficit, and counts of sick days, child-sick days, and holidays for the month.

Yearly statistics

Zoom out to see the full year. The yearly cumulative balance shows whether you've worked more or less than contracted over the entire year to date.

Year selector

Use the year selector at the top of the History tab to look at previous years. Your full history is stored as long as your account is active.

Key Concept

Understanding Your Balance

The balance is the difference between what you worked and what you owe. A positive balance means overtime; a negative balance means you still owe hours.

How the balance is calculated

  1. For each day, TapShift sums all worked minutes from your time logs.
  2. It fetches the due minutes for that day from your target (accounting for weekends and public holidays).
  3. The daily balance is worked − due.
  4. Monthly and yearly balances sum all daily balances and add a cumulative carry-forward from previous periods.
  5. The running total you see is your cumulative balance from the start of your earliest target to today.
Live updates: While a timer is running, the balance updates every minute. You can see your overtime or deficit grow in real time on the Timer tab.
Offline Mode

Offline Mode and Sync

TapShift stores all your data locally in your browser first. You can start and stop timers, add entries, and view your balance without any internet connection. When the app detects a connection, it syncs with the server automatically.

Sync uses a conflict resolution system based on the updated_at timestamp. The most recently modified version of any record wins. You'll be notified if a conflict occurs (rare) and can choose which version to keep.

Note: If you use TapShift on multiple devices, make sure to sync (open the app and wait for the loading indicator to clear) on the first device before switching to another to avoid conflicts.
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use TapShift on multiple devices?
Yes. Log in with the same account on your phone and laptop. Data syncs automatically whenever both devices have an internet connection.
My balance looks wrong — what should I do?
Check the following:
  • Is your target start date correct? Balances are calculated from that date.
  • Is the correct state/country set in Settings?
  • Are public holidays excluded in your target settings?
  • Try the Recalculate button in Settings if balances seem stale.
What happens if a timer runs past midnight?
TapShift handles cross-midnight shifts correctly. The effective worked time is split between the two days, and break deduction is applied only once (at the end of the shift, on the last day).
Can I export my data?
Data export is on the roadmap. For now, your data is stored in your account and synced to the server. You can also self-host the app from the GitHub repository and access the database directly.
Is my data private?
Your time logs are stored on the TapShift server (a VPS operated by the independent developer). There is no analytics, no third-party tracking, and no data sharing. The source code is open — you can verify this on GitHub.
Do I need to configure anything for German break law?
Just enable Apply break calculation on your work buttons. TapShift takes care of the rest automatically.
Where can I report a bug or request a feature?
Open an issue on GitHub. The developer reads and responds to all issues.

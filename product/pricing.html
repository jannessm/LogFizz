Why does TapShift cost money at all?

Servers, domain names, SSL certificates, email delivery, and database backups all cost real money every month. TapShift is built and maintained by a single independent developer — not a funded startup. The €5/year subscription covers those costs and nothing more. There are no shareholders expecting returns, no growth targets, and no plan to raise prices and cash out.

If running costs ever drop significantly, the price will drop too. If you genuinely cannot afford the subscription, reach out on GitHub — we'll work something out.

FAQ

Pricing questions

Do I need a credit card to start the free trial?
No. Just create an account and start using TapShift immediately. You'll only be asked for payment details at the end of the 2-month trial period.
What happens when my trial ends?
You'll be prompted to subscribe for €5/year. Your data is preserved. If the paywall is disabled by the administrator at that time, you'll continue to have full access. You can also cancel and export your data at any time.
Can I cancel my subscription?
Yes, at any time from the Settings page inside the app. You'll retain access until the end of the paid year.
Is there a team or multi-seat plan?
Not currently. TapShift is designed for individuals tracking their own hours. Each user pays €5/year for their own account.
Will the price go up?
Not without advance notice. The price is set to cover costs. If infrastructure gets cheaper, the price may go down. Any increase would be communicated well in advance and existing subscribers would be grandfathered at their current rate for the remainder of their subscription period.
Where does the €5 go?
It covers:
  • VPS / server hosting
  • PostgreSQL & Redis database instances
  • Domain name and SSL certificates
  • Email delivery (for password resets and balance reports)
  • Stripe payment processing fees
There is no salary drawn from subscription revenue by the developer.
Is TapShift open source?
Yes. The full source code is available on GitHub. You're welcome to self-host it for free.
